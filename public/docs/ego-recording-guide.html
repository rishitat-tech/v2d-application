EGO Recording Guide

This guide explains the main recording expectations for EGO tasks, including movement quality, safety, stability, and what to avoid during collection.

Rule of Thumb

When recording, prioritize safety and smooth, deliberate movements above all else. Treat the hardware with care and do not deliberately force anything.

Key Principles

  • Go slow: Avoid fast or jerky movements that can cause errors or auto-deletions.
  • Use natural grasp: Grasp actions should be smooth, consistent, natural, and easy to interpret.
  • Keep consistent speed: Maintain a steady pace throughout the task. Do not only slow down in certain moments.
  • Use smooth continuous movement: Movements should be fluid and related to the task, without unnecessary pauses.
  • Minimize waist rotation: Avoid unnecessary rotation that makes the video unstable.
  • Avoid making a fist: When not interacting with objects, keep fingers relaxed and visible.

What to Avoid

  • Fast movements.
  • Jerky or unexpected motion.
  • Unnecessary pauses.
  • Unrelated movements that are not part of the task.
  • Excessive waist rotation.
  • Clenching the hand into a fist when not interacting with objects.

Quick Checklist

  • Move slowly and deliberately.
  • Keep motion smooth and continuous.
  • Use natural grasping behavior.
  • Keep speed consistent.
  • Keep fingers relaxed and visible when not interacting with objects.
  • Do not force hardware or objects.

Good EGO recordings should look stable, natural, and easy to understand from the head-mounted camera view.
