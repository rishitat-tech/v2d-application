MV Calibration Guide

This guide explains how to prepare for MV calibration, position the calibration board, perform the movement, and verify the calibration video before starting data collection.

Purpose of Calibration

The calibration board and calibration process are used for extrinsic calibration, which helps determine the 3D camera pose. This assumes the individual cameras already have correctly calibrated intrinsics.

Before Starting

  • Use a calibration board that meets the expected requirements.
  • Stand in the center of the marked bounding area.
  • Make sure all cameras are secure and not moving.
  • Ensure no other chessboard patterns are visible to any camera.
  • Confirm the calibration board is clearly visible to the cameras.

How to Move the Board

  • Move the board slowly and smoothly.
  • Avoid fast or jerky movements.
  • Keep the board vertical at camera height.
  • Slow, smooth motion helps reduce motion blur.

Calibration Movement Steps

  1. Start by facing each camera.
  2. Move slowly while turning toward the left.
  3. Hold the board vertically at camera height.
  4. Perform a smooth stationary full rotation for about 20–30 seconds.
  5. Step back about 1.5 meters, or about 5 feet.
  6. Circle around the area while moving the board up and down for about 30 seconds.
  7. Once complete, inform the person at the computer so the process can be closed.

After Calibration

Once calibration is complete, verify that the calibration video is good quality before starting data collection.

Calibration recordings can be checked at:
/mnt/nova_ssd/recordings/<recording_name>

Verifying the calibration video increases the chances of collecting high-quality data.

Things to Avoid

  • Do not move the board too quickly.
  • Do not allow cameras to move during calibration.
  • Do not allow other chessboard patterns to be visible.
  • Do not start data collection until the calibration video has been verified.

If a camera moves during or after calibration, recalibration may be needed before collecting data.

Quick Checklist

  • Calibration board ready.
  • Person/board centered in the marked bounding area.
  • Cameras secure and not moving.
  • No extra chessboard patterns visible.
  • Board moved slowly and smoothly.
  • Full rotation completed.
  • Step-back and circle movement completed.
  • Calibration video verified before data collection.
