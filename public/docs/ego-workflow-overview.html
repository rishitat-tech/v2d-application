EGO Workflow Overview

This overview explains the EGO recording workflow at a high level, including setup, operator checks, data collector expectations, gesture requirements, and recording quality.

Purpose

EGO recording captures tasks from a head-mounted camera view. The goal is to collect clear, stable, and usable recordings where the task, hands, wrists, and object interactions remain visible.

Main Workflow

  1. Prepare the head-mounted camera and recording setup.
  2. Operator confirms the camera view is clear.
  3. Data collector starts with the required hand pose.
  4. Data collector holds the pose steadily for 3 seconds.
  5. Data collector performs the task slowly, naturally, and consistently.
  6. Operator watches that hands, wrists, and objects remain in frame.
  7. Data collector returns to the required start pose at the end.
  8. Operator ends the recording and confirms whether the sequence is usable.

Key Quality Rules

  • Hands and wrists should stay visible.
  • No extra hands or people should appear in the camera view.
  • Movements should be slow, smooth, natural, and deliberate.
  • Avoid fast, jerky, or unrelated movements.
  • Keep the head-mounted camera view stable.
  • Do not force hardware or objects.
