EGO Data Collection Guide

This guide covers both operator and data collector responsibilities for EGO recording.

Data Collector Responsibilities

  • Wear and use the head-mounted camera setup correctly.
  • Start with palms facing each other and fingers bent.
  • Hold the start pose steadily for 3 seconds.
  • Keep fingers and wrists visible in the camera view.
  • Perform the task slowly, naturally, and consistently.
  • Avoid jerky, fast, or unrelated movements.
  • Avoid making a fist when not actively interacting with objects.
  • End with the same hand pose and hold it for 3 seconds.

Operator Responsibilities

  • Confirm the camera view is clear before starting.
  • Make sure hands, wrists, and the task interaction are visible.
  • Make sure no other person or extra hand is visible in frame.
  • Watch that the task stays in frame throughout the recording.
  • Check that the data collector keeps movement slow and stable.
  • If hands, wrists, or objects go out of frame, delete the sequence and redo it.
  • End the recording once the task and end pose are complete.

Movement Expectations

  • Prioritize safety and smooth, deliberate movement.
  • Use natural grasping techniques.
  • Keep speed consistent throughout the task.
  • Use fluid, continuous movement without unnecessary pauses.
  • Minimize waist rotation so the video stays stable.
  • Treat the hardware with care and do not force anything.

Quick Checklist

  • Camera view clear.
  • Hands and wrists visible.
  • No extra hands or people in frame.
  • Start pose held for 3 seconds.
  • Task performed slowly and naturally.
  • Task interaction stays in frame.
  • End pose held for 3 seconds.

If important parts of the task leave the frame, the sequence should be deleted and redone.
