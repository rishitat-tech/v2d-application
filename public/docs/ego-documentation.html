EGO Guide

EGO Documentation

This documentation summarizes the EGO recording workflow, data collector expectations, operator checks, gesture requirements, and quality rules.

Purpose

EGO recording captures tasks from a head-mounted camera view. The recording should clearly show the hands, wrists, objects, and task interaction.

Main Rules

  • Prioritize safety and smooth, deliberate movements.
  • Treat the hardware with care and do not force anything.
  • Start with the required hand pose and return to that pose at the end.
  • Hold the start pose for 3 seconds.
  • Keep fingers and wrists visible.
  • Make sure no extra person or extra hand is visible in the head-mounted camera view.
  • Keep the task interaction in frame.

Movement Quality

  • Move slowly and naturally.
  • Use smooth, continuous movements.
  • Keep speed consistent throughout the task.
  • Avoid jerky, fast, or unexpected movements.
  • Avoid unnecessary waist rotation.
  • Do not make a fist when not interacting with objects.

Movement Principles

Law 1: Deliberate Over Fast

  • Speed is not a quality metric.
  • A slow, clean trajectory is more valuable than a fast, noisy one.
  • Prioritize clear, controlled movement over speed.

Law 2: Smooth Over Segmented

  • Use continuous, fluid motions instead of stop-start patterns.
  • Avoid micro-adjustments in the middle of the movement.
  • Plan the movement path before executing the action.

Law 3: Consistent Over Creative

  • Repeat the same movement pattern across episodes when possible.
  • Avoid unnecessary variation in how the task is performed.
  • Consistent approaches help reduce variation in the collected data.

Movement Speed & Duration

Target duration is 10–40 seconds minimum per episode, unless stated otherwise.

  • Teaching Speed: Slowest speed with exaggerated clarity. This is the default for all collection.
  • Standard Speed: Measured and purposeful pace. Use when specified.
  • Natural Speed: Real-world practical speed. Use only when explicitly requested.
