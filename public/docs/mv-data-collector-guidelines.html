MV Data Collector Guidelines

This guide explains where the data collector should stand, how they should perform the task, and what they should or should not wear during MV data collection.

Where the Data Collector Should Stand

  • Start inside the marked bounding area.
  • Remain inside the marked bounding area for the entire sequence.
  • Do not step outside the bounding area during the task.
  • Make sure the object and data collector are visible to the cameras.
  • At the start of each sequence, the target object should not be blocked from any camera view.

How to Perform the Task

  • Wait for the operator to start recording first.
  • Begin moving after the operator gives the countdown.
  • Move slowly, naturally, and consistently.
  • Avoid fast, jerky, or sudden movements.
  • Keep a consistent speed throughout the sequence.
  • Follow the action description for the assigned task.
  • Notify the operator when the sequence is complete.

Each sequence is expected to be about 30 seconds. Some variation is acceptable, but following the action description takes priority.

What to Wear

Wear:

  • Fitted clothing.
  • Comfortable footwear that allows safe and controlled movement.
  • Hair tied back or kept above shoulder length if needed.

What Not to Wear

Avoid:

  • Baggy clothing.
  • Loose tops.
  • Shorts.
  • Skirts.
  • Dresses.
  • High heels.
  • Chunky footwear.
  • Scarves or loose accessories.
  • Anything that blocks the object, hands, or body movement from the camera view.

Badge Requirement

  • Always remove the badge before starting the sequence.
  • Do not keep the badge hanging or visible during the task.
  • Remove anything that may swing, block the view, or interfere with capture quality.

Quick Checklist

  • Inside marked bounding area.
  • Object visible.
  • Fitted clothing.
  • No badge.
  • No baggy/loose clothing.
  • No shorts, skirts, or dresses.
  • No high heels or chunky footwear.
  • Slow, natural, consistent movements.
