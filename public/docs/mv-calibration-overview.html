MV Calibration

Calibration Overview

This overview explains why calibration is needed before MV data collection. For full steps, use the MV Calibration Guide.

Why Calibration Is Used

Calibration helps align the camera views and improves the quality of MV data collection. It supports extrinsic camera pose calibration, which helps the system understand where each camera is positioned in 3D space.

What Calibration Uses

  • A calibration board that meets the required expectations.
  • Secure cameras that are not moving.
  • A clear marked bounding area.
  • Slow and smooth board movement to reduce motion blur.
  • Good lighting so the calibration board is clearly visible to all cameras.

Calibration Board Example

Calibration Steps

Position the Person / Board

  • Stand in the center of the marked area.

Check the Cameras

  • Make sure all cameras are secure and not moving.
  • Ensure there are no other chessboard patterns visible to any camera.

Move the Board Slowly

  • Use slow, smooth motions to help reduce motion blur.

Calibration Movement

  1. Start by turning to face each camera.
  2. Move slowly while turning toward the left.
  3. Hold the board vertically at camera height.
  4. Complete a smooth, stationary full rotation for about 20–30 seconds.
  5. Step back about 1.5 meters, or about 5 feet.
  6. Circle around the area while moving the board up and down for about 30 seconds.

Finish

  • Once done, inform the person at the computer so the process can be closed.

Before Data Collection

After calibration is complete, the calibration video should be checked for quality before starting data collection. This increases the chance of collecting clean and high-quality data.
